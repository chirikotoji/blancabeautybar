Frequently asked

Before you book.

How is the Russian manicure different from a regular gel manicure? +

Regular gel manicures soak the nail and cut the cuticle, then apply polish. The Russian technique is dry, we use a professional e-file to refine the cuticle and apply the gel flush against the skin. That's why our sets last 4–5 weeks instead of 2.

Is the e-file safe? +

When done by a trained therapist, the e-file is gentler on the cuticle than traditional cutting. Both B and Maryam are trained specifically in the Russian e-file method, it's the technique we've used for seven-plus years on Clarence Street. Every metal tool is autoclave-sterilized between clients (hospital standard); e-file bits and abrasives are single-use.

How are your tools cleaned between clients? +

Every reusable tool we use is autoclave-sterilized between clients, the same standard hospitals use for surgical instruments. An autoclave kills all microbes (including spores) by sealing tools in pressurized steam at over 121°C, which is a higher bar than the disinfectant soak most salons rely on. Single-use abrasives like e-file bits and buffers are disposed of after each appointment. It's why a recurring word in our reviews is "clean." Hygiene isn't a feature we mention, it's the baseline we don't compromise on.

How long does a Russian manicure last? +

Four to five weeks for most clients. Because the gel sits flush to the skin from day one, lift at the cuticle is rare, most clients tell us their nails still look freshly done at the four-week mark.

Can I bring inspiration photos? +

Please do. We'll talk through length, shape, finish and colour at the start of your appointment to make sure we're aligned. With 250+ gel colours on the wall, almost anything is on the table.

Is the laser safe for darker skin tones? +

Yes. We use a diode laser, the only technology proven to work safely across every skin type, including darker tones (Fitzpatrick IV–VI) where older lasers like IPL and alexandrite carry risk. Most clients describe the sensation as warm, not painful. A free 15-minute consultation comes first to plan the right course for your skin and hair.

How far out are you booking? +

Saturdays book first, usually 1–2 weeks out. Weekday slots are typically available within the week. Live availability is on Fresha.

Do you take walk-ins? +

Occasionally, but the studio is by appointment and Saturdays go first. Booking on Fresha is the safest way to lock in your slot.
