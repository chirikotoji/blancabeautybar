Chapter One · The Signature

The Russian E-File
Manicure.

A dry, electric-file technique perfected in Eastern Europe — taken seriously by a handful of studios in Canada. One of them is on Clarence Street.

We don't paint over overgrown cuticle. We file it back to the natural seal, prep the plate, and lay a finish that refuses to lift. The difference shows on day 19.

01

Prep — a sterile, dry cuticle.

Medical-grade e-file with sterilized bits. Pterygium removed to the eponychium without water bath, without nipping live skin.

02

Build — architecture, not paint.

A featherweight gel base sculpts an apex on every nail. The structural arch is what carries you through three weeks of life.

03

Finish — mirror gloss, every time.

Two coats, cured under medical LED. A topcoat that won't yellow under sun, won't dull from dishwater.

Chapter Four · Why Blanca

Three decisions that add up to a different chair.

01

We file. We don't soak.

Water-bath manicures swell the cuticle and lift polish on day seven. The Russian dry technique seals the plate so the gel hugs the cuticle for nineteen days, sometimes more.

02

A laser for every skin tone.

The diode platform we use is rated for Fitzpatrick I through VI. Cool-tip handpiece. No icing required. Most clients describe a snap rather than a burn.

03

We see you on time.

Two chairs. No double-booking. We've kept a 99.4% on-time rate since opening — your appointment starts when it's supposed to and ends when it's supposed to.
