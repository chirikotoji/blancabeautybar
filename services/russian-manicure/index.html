The Russian manicure.

A dry e-file technique. Cuticles refined, never cut. Gel applied flush against the skin. Four to five weeks of wear.

The technique

Done
properly.

The Russian e-file technique is performed dry, with no soaking. The cuticle is gently lifted and refined using a professional rotary tool, never cut. The gel is then applied flush against the skin, sealed at the cuticle line so it cannot lift away.

The result is a manicure that holds for four to five weeks before the next appointment, not two. Both Buthyna and Maryam have trained specifically in this method since the studio opened in 2019.

Every metal tool at the station is autoclave-sterilized between clients. Single-use abrasives are disposed of after each appointment.

Before you book.

How is this different from a regular gel manicure? +

Regular gel manicures soak the nail and cut the cuticle, then apply polish. The Russian e-file technique is dry, the cuticle is refined gently with a professional tool, and the gel sits flush against the skin. That's why it lasts four to five weeks instead of two.

Is the e-file safe? +

When done by a trained therapist, the e-file is gentler on the cuticle than traditional cutting. Both Buthyna and Maryam have been trained specifically in the Russian method since the studio opened in 2019. Every metal tool is autoclave-sterilized between clients.

How long does a Russian manicure last? +

Four to five weeks for most clients. Because the gel sits flush to the skin from day one, lift at the cuticle is rare. Most clients still see a fresh-looking finish at the four-week mark.

Can I bring inspiration photos? +

Please do. We'll talk through length, shape, finish and colour at the start of your appointment. With 250+ gel colours on the wall, almost anything is possible.
